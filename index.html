Exercise 5: Grammar Spotlight & Detective Notebook

Grammar Rule: Infinitives of Purpose

We use the Infinitive of Purpose (to + base verb) to explain why someone does something or the purpose of an action.

  • Incorrect: Amina sat under the trees for finding peace.
  • Correct: Amina sat under the trees to find peace.

Tip: Test your sentence by replacing "to" with "in order to". If it sounds right, you've mastered it!
